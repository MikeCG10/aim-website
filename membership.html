Membership

Open to all currently enrolled CSUSB students. Here's how membership and mentorship work in AIM.

Membership categories

Every member falls into one of three categories.

Mentee Member

Seeking guidance, training, and support in AI, InfoSec, government cybersecurity frameworks, career development, or competition prep.

  • Attend meetings, trainings, and mentorship activities
  • Communicate goals and progress with a mentor
  • Participate in approved competitions when available

Mentor Member

Has experience or skills in AI, InfoSec, frameworks, competitions, or career development and supports Mentee members.

  • Approved through a mentor interest form + Cabinet meeting
  • Serves as a general mentor or a specific niche mentor
  • Helps track mentee progress and mentorship goals

Associate Member

CSUSB faculty, staff, alumni, or community members who support AIM's mission.

  • May participate in meetings and activities
  • Cannot vote or hold elected office
  • Capped at 20% of total membership

Mentor categories

Mentors don't need to be experts in everything — they're matched by strength and focus area.

Technical Mentor

Hands-on cybersecurity, AI, InfoSec labs, tools, and technical skill development.

Competition Mentor

Preparation for IE Mayor's Cyber Cup, NCAE Cyber Games, CCDC, NCL, CPTC, and other CAE Atlas competitions.

Framework Mentor

NIST Cybersecurity Framework 2.0, NIST SP 800-53, and other government, industry, or compliance frameworks.

Career Mentor

Resumes, interviews, internships, networking, and cybersecurity career pathways.

Research Mentor

Cybersecurity or AI research, project planning, technical writing, and presentations.

General Mentor

Broad guidance, organization involvement, beginner questions, and study planning.

What's expected of members

All members

Participate respectfully, support AIM's mission, follow the Constitution and CSUSB policies, and help maintain a positive, professional, inclusive environment.

Mentees

Attend mentorship activities when available, communicate with mentors about goals and progress, and participate in competitions when possible.

Mentors

Provide guidance and encouragement, assist with workshops and competition prep, and help track mentee progress through basic mentorship metrics.

Ready to join?

Mentee, Mentor, or Associate — there's a place for you in AIM.
